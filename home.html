SCOUTING

Scouting is a worldwide youth movement that helps young people develop leadership, discipline, teamwork, and practical life skills through outdoor activities and community service. It focuses on shaping responsible citizens who can positively contribute to society.

Scouting began in 1907 when Lord Baden-Powell organized the first experimental camp on Brownsea Island in the United Kingdom. The movement then spread rapidly across the world, becoming a global youth development program.

KENYA SCOUTS ASSOCIATION

The Kenya Scouts Association is the official body responsible for scouting activities in Kenya. It guides and supports scouts in schools and communities through training, camps, and service projects such as environmental conservation, first aid, and leadership development.

Scouting in Kenya started during the colonial period and grew steadily over time. After independence, it was formally organized under the Kenya Scouts Association to coordinate and expand scouting activities nationwide in line with global standards set by the World Organization of the Scout Movement.

KASARANI SUBCOUNTY SCOUTS

The Kasarani Sub County Scouts Association is a local scouting body in Nairobi that organizes and supports scouting activities in schools and youth groups within Kasarani. It helps young people develop leadership, discipline, teamwork, and service skills through training, camps, and community projects like cleaning, tree planting, and first aid
It developed as part of the growth of the Kenya Scouts Association, expanding scouting activities to sub-county level to reach more schools and communities in Kasarani
